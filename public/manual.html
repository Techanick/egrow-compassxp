1. Overview

Manager Connect is a companion app for the Expanscience Management Framework. It helps managers:

  • Explore the 4 Roles, 6 Skills, and 96 Behaviors of the management framework
  • Conduct periodic self-assessments against framework behaviors
  • Build and track personalized development plans
  • Receive timely reminders aligned with the performance cycle

For HR and supervisors, the app also provides team oversight, assessment review, and aggregated skill analytics.

2. Getting Started

2.1 Creating Your Account

  1. Navigate to the application URL
  2. Click "Sign Up" on the login page
  3. Enter your full name, email address, and a secure password
  4. Check your inbox for a verification email and click the confirmation link
  5. Return to the app and sign in with your credentials

2.2 Signing In

  1. Go to the login page
  2. Enter your email and password
  3. Click "Sign In"

2.3 Resetting Your Password

  1. On the login page, click "Forgot Password?"
  2. Enter your registered email address
  3. Follow the link in the password reset email
  4. Set a new password and sign in
💡 Tip: Choose a strong password with at least 8 characters, including uppercase, lowercase, and a number.

3. Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home screen after signing in. It provides an at-a-glance summary of your management development journey.

3.1 Periodic Reminders

At the top of the Dashboard, contextual reminder cards appear based on your activity:

Reminder Trigger Urgency
No assessment completed You have never submitted a self-assessment 🔴 Urgent
Annual review due Last assessment was more than 270 days ago 🔴 Urgent
Quarterly check-in Last assessment was more than 90 days ago 🟡 Warning
No development plan You completed an assessment but have no plan 🟡 Warning
Plan needs update Development plan not updated in 30+ days 🔵 Info / 🔴 Urgent (90+ days)

Click any reminder card to navigate directly to the relevant section.

3.2 Quick Actions

Three shortcut cards let you jump to common tasks:

  • Start Assessment — begin or continue a self-assessment
  • Update Plan — view or modify your development plan
  • Browse Framework — explore skills, roles, and behaviors

3.3 Progress Summary (Radar Chart)

A radar/spider chart shows your current mastery level across the 6 management skills. Each axis represents one skill, with levels from 0 (no data) to 4 (Referent/Role Model).

The chart updates automatically when you submit a new self-assessment.

The Framework Explorer is your interactive reference for the Expanscience Management Framework.

4.1 Structure

4.2 Mastery Levels

Each skill has behaviors organized into four mastery levels:

  • Fundamentals — Essential baseline behaviors
  • Intermediate — Developing proficiency
  • Advanced — Strong, consistent practice
  • Referent / Role Model — Exemplary, coaching others

4.3 Development Tools

Each skill links to recommended development tools (e.g., Insights Discovery, NVC, SMART Objectives). Click a tool name to see its description, format, and when to use it.

5. Self-Assessment

5.1 How It Works

  1. Navigate to Self-Assessment from the sidebar
  2. For each of the 6 skills, you'll see behaviors grouped by mastery level
  3. Rate how often you observe each behavior in yourself:
    • Hardly ever
    • Sometimes
    • Often
    • Almost always
  4. Click "Submit Assessment" when finished

5.2 Level Validation Logic

A mastery level is validated when at least 3 behaviors at that level are rated "Often" or "Almost always."

💡 Tip: Be honest in your self-assessment. The goal is development, not evaluation. Accurate ratings lead to more meaningful development plans.

5.3 Assessment History

All your past assessments are saved and accessible from the assessment page. You can compare your progress over time.

5.4 Recommended Frequency

  • Annual — Full assessment before the Q3 development interview (mandatory)
  • Quarterly — Quick check-in to track progress (recommended)

6. Development Plan

6.1 Creating a Plan

  1. After completing a self-assessment, navigate to Development Plan
  2. The app recommends 1–2 skills to focus on based on your assessment gaps
  3. Select target skills, target mastery level, and specific behaviors to work on
  4. For each behavior, define concrete development actions:
    • Training or workshop attendance
    • Reading or e-learning
    • Feedback sessions with peers
    • Co-development groups
    • Coaching engagements
  5. Set deadlines aligned with your performance cycle

6.2 Tracking Progress

Mark actions as in progress or completed as you advance. The Dashboard radar chart will reflect your improvements after your next self-assessment.

⚠️ Important: Review and update your development plan at least once a month to stay on track.

7. Team Management

The Team section is available for users with supervisor or HR roles.

7.1 Supervisor View

  • View direct reports' self-assessment results and development plans
  • Review and validate assessments during the Q3 development interview
  • See an aggregated team view to identify collective development needs

7.2 Managing Team Members

  • Add team members by email from the Team page
  • Remove team members when reporting lines change

7.3 HR View

HR users can see all managers across the organization, assign roles, and access broader analytics.

8. Settings

Access Settings from the sidebar to manage your profile and preferences:

  • Full Name — Update your display name
  • Department — Select your department from the organizational structure
  • Geography — Set your geographic zone
  • Language — Choose your preferred language (see section 9)
  • Notification Preferences — Toggle reminders for assessments and plan updates

10. FAQ & Troubleshooting

Q: How often should I complete a self-assessment?

A: At minimum once per year before the Q3 development interview. Quarterly check-ins are recommended for continuous development tracking.

Q: Can I edit a submitted assessment?

A: No, but you can complete a new assessment at any time. Your history is preserved so you can track your evolution over time.

Q: What does it mean when a mastery level is "validated"?

A: A level is validated when you rate at least 3 behaviors at that level as "Often" or "Almost always." This indicates consistent practice of those behaviors.

Q: I don't see the Team section in my sidebar.

A: The Team section is only visible to users with a supervisor or HR role. Contact your HR administrator to update your role if needed.

Q: Can my supervisor see my self-assessment?

A: Yes. If you are assigned as a direct report to a supervisor, they can view your assessments and development plans to facilitate the development interview.

Q: How do I change my password?

A: Sign out, then click "Forgot Password?" on the login page and follow the email instructions.
